GO Enrichment analysis

Gene Ontology enrichment analysis (GOEA) leverage the structured vocabulary of Gene Ontology (GO) to statistically identify overrepresented biological terms within these gene sets. By comparing the observed distribution of GO terms with a reference dataset, GOEA unveils the underlying biological context of genes, help pinpointing the key processes, cellular components, and molecular functions associated with their data.

Traditionally performed on extant genomes, this analysis identifies functions that are over-represented in a study set (foreground) compared to the population (background). We now facilitate Gene Ontology Enrichment Analyses (GOEA) for sets of both extant genes or ancestral genes. Extant gene GO enrichment is carried out as described in GOATOOLS, where the study set is a user-defined set of extant genes, in the population of all genes in the extant genome of interest. For the ancestral gene GO enrichment, we exploit the HOG GO annotations by considering a study set of user-defined HOGs at a given taxonomic level, compared to the population of all HOGs defined at a given taxonomic level.
